BMW M4 Competition

Engine and Performance

The BMW M4 Competition is powered by a 3.0-liter twin-turbo inline-6 engine (S58), delivering 503 horsepower and 650 Nm (479 lb-ft) of torque. It’s mated to an 8-speed M Steptronic automatic transmission, with power sent either to the rear wheels (RWD) or to all four wheels (xDrive version), depending on the model variant.

  • 0-62 mph (0-100 km/h): 3.5 seconds (with xDrive)
  • Top Speed: 250 km/h (155 mph), up to 290 km/h (180 mph) with M Driver’s Package

Design and Aerodynamics

The M4 Competition features an aggressive and divisive design, especially with its large vertical kidney grilles. It includes M-specific bumpers, flared wheel arches, functional air intakes, and a carbon fiber roof. Aerodynamics are optimized for both cooling and downforce, with a rear diffuser and subtle lip spoiler.

Interior and Technology

The M4 uses a stiffened version of BMW’s modular CLAR platform. It features adaptive M suspension, M-tuned differential, and optional carbon ceramic brakes. The M4 Competition with xDrive offers impressive cornering traction with rear-biased behavior and selectable drive modes.
Inside, the cockpit includes M Carbon bucket seats (optional), two large digital screens (12.3” instrument cluster and 10.25” iDrive touchscreen), and driver-focused controls. The driving experience is enhanced by configurable M modes and track-specific telemetry tools.
