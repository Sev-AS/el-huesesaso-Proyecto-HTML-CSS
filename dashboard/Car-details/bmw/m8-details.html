BMW M8 Competition

Engine and Performance

At the heart of the BMW M8 Competition is a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine (S63) that produces 617 horsepower and 750 Nm (553 lb-ft) of torque. Power is delivered through an 8-speed M Steptronic automatic transmission with xDrive all-wheel drive, allowing for exceptional traction and launch control. The M8 is capable of switching between AWD and RWD for a more dynamic experience.

  • 0-62 mph (0-100 km/h): 3.2 seconds
  • Top Speed: 250 km/h (155 mph) electronically limited (can be raised to 305 km/h (190 mph) with the optional M Driver’s Package)

Design and Aerodynamics

The M8 Competition blends luxury and aggression, with muscular proportions and aerodynamic refinements. It features M-specific bumpers, a sculpted hood, large air intakes, and a subtle rear spoiler. The coupe retains BMW’s elegant lines while integrating performance cues like quad exhausts, carbon fiber roof, and active aerodynamics for improved high-speed stability.

Interior and Technology

The M8 is built on BMW’s CLAR architecture, with enhancements for stiffness and dynamics. The chassis includes adaptive M suspension, M Servotronic steering, and M Carbon Ceramic brakes (optional). The xDrive AWD system offers rear-bias handling, with a 2WD mode for purists.
Inside, the cabin is refined and tech-laden, with Merino leather upholstery, carbon fiber trims, and dual displays: a 12.3" instrument cluster and a 10.25" central touchscreen. It also features M mode settings for customizable driving dynamics.
