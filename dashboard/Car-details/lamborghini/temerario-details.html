Lamborghini Temerario

Engine and Performance

At the heart of the Temerario is a new 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine with a flat-plane crankshaft, engineered and developed from the ground up in Sant'Agata Bolognese. This engine alone produces 789 horsepower and 538 lb-ft of torque. A key feature is its impressive 10,000 rpm redline. The V8 is paired with three electric motors. One is positioned between the engine and the eight-speed dual-clutch transmission, while the other two are located on the front axle, one for each wheel. This hybrid system brings the total combined output to 907 horsepower.

  • 0-62 mph (0-100 km/h): 2.7 seconds
  • Top Speed: Over 210 mph (340 km/h)

Design and Aerodynamics

The design of the Temerario, crafted by Lamborghini's Centro Stile, features iconic and essential lines that direct airflow to maximize cooling and downforce. Notable design elements include hexagonal daytime running lights and a "shark-nose" front end, contributing to its futuristic aesthetic.

Interior and Technology

Inside, the Temerario is designed to make the driver "feel like a pilot," with a low seating position, a slim dashboard, and an intuitively designed steering wheel. The car is equipped with an advanced Human-Machine Interface (HMI) that includes a 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster, an 8.4-inch central infotainment display, and a 9.1-inch passenger-side screen.
