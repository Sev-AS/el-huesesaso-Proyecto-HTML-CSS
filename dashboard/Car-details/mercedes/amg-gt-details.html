Mercedes-AMG GT

Mercedes-AMG GT Specifications

Engine and Performance

The hallmark of the AMG GT is its handcrafted engine, built according to AMG's "One Man, One Engine" philosophy. The first generation primarily featured a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine with power outputs ranging from 456 hp in the base GT to 720 hp in the Black Series. The second generation offers a range of engines, from a 2.0-liter inline-four in the GT 43 to the potent twin-turbo 4.0-liter V8 in the GT 55 and GT 63 models. The top-of-the-line GT 63 S E PERFORMANCE plug-in hybrid boasts a system output of 816 hp.

Design and Features

The AMG GT is characterized by its long hood, a cabin set far back, and a wide, muscular rear. This design is a modern interpretation of the classic grand tourer silhouette. The interior is driver-focused, with a cockpit-like feel, and features high-quality materials like leather, carbon fiber, and aluminum. Later models are equipped with advanced technology, including large digital displays for the instrument cluster and infotainment system.

Generations and Key Model Variants

The first generation of the AMG GT (C190/R190) was produced from 2014 to 2023. The second generation (C192) was introduced in 2023 for the 2024 model year. A significant change for this generation is the standard inclusion of an all-wheel-drive system (4MATIC+).
