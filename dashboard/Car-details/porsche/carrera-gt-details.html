Engine and Performance

At the heart of the Carrera GT is a naturally aspirated 5.7-liter V10 engine, originally developed for motorsport. This high-revving engine produces 612 horsepower and 435 lb-ft (590 Nm) of torque, sending all power to the rear wheels through a 6-speed manual transmission with a ceramic clutch. With no turbocharging or hybrid assistance, the Carrera GT delivers a pure analog driving experience, making it one of the most engaging supercars ever built.

  • 0-62 mph (0-100 km/h): 3.9 seconds
  • Top Speed: 330 km/h (205 mph)

Design and Aerodynamics

The Carrera GT's design is timeless, with a low, wide stance and clean aerodynamic lines. It features a carbon fiber monocoque chassis and a large, manually operated rear wing that deploys at high speeds to enhance stability. The bodywork emphasizes functionality, including large air intakes for cooling the mid-mounted V10. While it doesn’t have scissor doors, the Carrera GT’s sleek silhouette and exposed carbon fiber touches reflect Porsche’s commitment to lightweight performance engineering.

Interior and Technology

Built around a carbon fiber monocoque and subframe, the Carrera GT was one of the first road cars to extensively use carbon-reinforced plastic in its structure. This gives it incredible stiffness and low weight (~1,380 kg dry). Its double wishbone suspension and ceramic brakes provide precise handling and fade-free stopping power. Unlike modern supercars, the Carrera GT is entirely mechanical—no stability control, no ABS interventions, and no power steering assistance at high speeds, offering a raw, demanding driving experience.
The interior is minimalist and driver-focused, with analog dials and physical controls. There are no digital screens, and every detail—from the birchwood gear knob to the exposed carbon fiber—emphasizes purity and performance.
