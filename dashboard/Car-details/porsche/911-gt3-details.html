Porsche-911-GT3-RS

Engine and Performance

At the heart of the 911 GT3 RS (992.1) is a 4.0-liter naturally aspirated flat-six boxer engine, revving up to 9,000 rpm. It produces 525 hp (386 kW) and 465 Nm (343 lb-ft) of torque. Power is sent to the rear wheels through a 7-speed dual-clutch PDK transmission with shorter gear ratios than the regular GT3 for enhanced acceleration and track performance.

  • 0-62 mph (0-100 km/h): 3.2 seconds
  • Top Speed: 296 km/h (184 mph)

Design and Aerodynamics

The GT3 RS is purpose-built for the track. It features a massive swan-neck rear wing with active DRS (Drag Reduction System), a front splitter, large vented front fenders, NACA ducts, and rear diffuser and a redesigned hood and side air extractors for maximum downforce
It produces up to 860 kg of downforce at 285 km/h (1,895 lb at 177 mph)—an extraordinary figure for a road-legal car.

Interior and Technology

Built on the 992-generation chassis, the GT3 RS uses extensive carbon fiber and lightweight materials for stiffness and agility. It features a Four-wheel steering, a manually adjustable suspension geometry and a optional Weissach Package (adds magnesium wheels and carbon aero parts, reducing weight by 22 kg)
The car weighs approximately 1,450 kg (DIN) / 1,525 kg (EU) depending on configuration.
It also includes Porsche Active Suspension Management (PASM) tuned for high-speed circuit use.
